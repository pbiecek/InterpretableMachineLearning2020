Homework 4: Medical Expenditure Panel Survey¶

This is explanatory analysis of medical expenses related to medical treatments. This is a regression problem. In this work I trained and compared 2 models:

  • Gradient Boosting Regressor
  • Linear Regression

Data preprocessing was done and categorical variables were One Hot Encoded. Health Expenses were also limited to maximum of 150k$.

Selected feature is the INCOME_M feature, which is a yearly income of some selected person.

The chart shows Ceteris Paribus for a selected observation 2, that is an instance with most overestimated value between prediction and predicted value. On the x side, it shows INCOME_M and y shows yearly expenses.

Yearly income of the patient starts to influence the outcome of the model, when > 200k yearly. Below that value the cost stays almost the same. That means, that until the patient earns less then 200k a year, his medical expenses are around 15 1k, but if he starts to earn more, the medical expenses would rise to 18k+.

2.2 Linear Regression¶

For this selected observation and linear regression model, the explanation is that the expenses don't change when INCOME_M or AGE31X changes. The reason for this might be the influence of other variables on the model outcome.

2.3 Gradient Boosting Regression¶

For the selected observation variable INCOME_M influences the model prediction, when the income is greater than 150k a year, the predicted cost rises from around 2k to almost 7k and more. With the increase of AGE31x after 50 the predicted cost also rises from 2k to 5k.

2.4 Summary¶

For this instance the influence of features INCOME_M and AGE31X is different between models. For Linear Regression there is no influence on the result, but for Gradient Boosting Regressor there is influence of those features on the predicted outcome. The reason for this might be how the two models learn and which variables the take into account when making predictions. For Linear Regression the outcome of the model does not change, while for Gradient Boosting Regressor they influence the outcome of the model.

def score_r2_print(clf, X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test):

    print('Test R^2:', r2_score(y_test, clf.predict(X_test)))
